mattdahlberg.yearbooks


A mobile experience to customize your student yearbook.

A yearbook marks a speacial time in life that you'll never forget and hopefully treasure forever.

Many students are not featured in thier schools yearbook because they either don't join clubs, don't play sports or participate in activities outside of school.

The Yearbooks feature within the Chatbooks app allow students to add photos from thier camera roll or socail media to few pages that will be added to a single copy of thier High School Yearbook just for them.

What is Chatbooks?

The Chatbooks App prints custom affordable photo books from your camera roll or instagram feed.

Mobile Design Challenge:

Design and present a new product idea and target audience within the chatbooks app.

How does it work?

The standard process is that Jr. High and High Schools produce the yearbook files gathered throughout the year to be submitted to a publisher. The Yearbooks feature with in the Chatbooks app enables every student to personally add thier own photos to a few additional pages from their camera roll or social media account and attactch them to a single copy of the schools files for the publisher.

The students will recieve a copy of a yearbook with a few additional pages created by themselves.

Ideation & Sketching

I began my process by sketching out the usesr's objectives and decisions in a Process Flow Map. I traditionally create user flow maps using software such as Viso, Lucidchart, etc., however I prefer the simplicity of pen and paper.

Also, sketching a few mockup screens always helps prepare my ideas for the wireframing and storyboarding process.

Conclusion

The product and demand appear viable and the design team loved the idea.

However the exteme challenge that was discovered is the variable printing process on the publisher end. The majority of profit comes from the thousands of copies printed for lower prices.
The price would sky rocket in manual labor to accomidate custome indivial pages into every book printed.

I haven't fully given up on this idea and someday I'll find a solution.
